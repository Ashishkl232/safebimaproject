Investment Plan

Investment insurance plan, as the name suggests, acts like an investment tool and provides you insurance coverage side by side. A part of the premium paid by you is utilized by the insurance company to provide investment opportunities in the life cover and the rest of the amount is invested in the instruments that are chosen by you. You can target long term goals or focus on short term investment plans depending upon personal reason for investment. Not only you get the security that your funds are safe and they are growing but you also get the most-needed protection which gives you the sense of relief that your dependents will be provided with the financial support that you are looking for them after you.

These investment plans are essentially of two types:

Unit Linked Insurance Plans or ULIPs

Traditional endowment plans

03

Tax Saving
Most of the plans today which are available in the market qualify for the tax deduction under Section 80C. Not only this, the lump sum amount received in the maturity of many plans is also Tax Free in nature under Section 10(10d). With this understanding the tax saving for any individual can vary from 10% to 33% depending on his/her income slab.

04

Returns
The returns offered are a good way to understand which plan is better which is not so certain a case with investment plans. Returns on investment plans are usually better if you consider post tax yields compared to other investment options (especially in Unit-Linked insurance Plans). Also, the returns in endowment plans only come after a few years while in the case of ULIPs, the insured can withdraw the entire amount once the lock in period has expired.

Concepts & FAQ's Investment Plans

What is a ULIP or Unit Linked Insurance Plan?
ULIPs stand for Unit Linked Insurance Products. In this investment instrument money paid as premium is invested into either equity or debt or a mix of the two. At the end of the policy term money is paid out to policyholder in accordance with the generated returns. In these policies a death cover is also provided to the policyholder.
How much tax can I save by investing in this policy?
There are two ways in which you will save tax. The amount that you have invested is eligible for deductions under 80C. In addition returns that you get from the policy are also tax free under section 10 (10 D)
How to invest money in ULIP online?
You will need a debit card, a credit card or netbanking. You can start a ULIP online through Safebima.com
What kind of different investment products are available?
Retail customers can choose between ULIPs, mutual funds, fixed deposits, equity, debt etc. Each of these vary in the risk and return profile. ULIPs and Mutual funds provide high returns - however cost of fund management is much lower for ULIPs (~1.35%) as compared to Mutual Funds (~2.25%). Debt instruments like FD, Traditional Plans provide you with secure and guaranteed returns.
Where will my money be invested?
Your money will be invested in a mix of equity and debt plans as per your choice. There are options available where switching between equity and debt funds will be done automtically on your behalf - depending on how the market is performing.
What payment modes are available?
You can pay through credit card, debit card, netbanking
Will I require a Demat account?
No, a demat account is not required for investing in these plans. This is a way to invest in the equity/debt market without having a demat account
Will I have a choice which fund my money gets invested into?
Yes, you can define which fund you want to choose. You can select the fund you want to invest in while filling the form.
